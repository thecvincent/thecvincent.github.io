UT Dallas Community Chorale
2014-2015

  • The UT Dallas Community Chorale (MUSI 2327, 1 credit hour)   The 50 - 90 voices Chorale offers students, alums and faculty, as well as members of the community, an opportunity to enjoy performing various pieces of choral music.  Community members are welcome to join the UT Dallas Community Chorale without having to register as a student at UT Dallas.

  • UT Dallas Community Chorale is non-auditioned, however musical training and background is advised.  The Chorale is open to all who are interested in singing music in a wide variety of styles from Medieval to modern pop.

  • The chorale meets once a week on Monday evenings from 7:00pm to 9:30pm.  The rehearsal hall is located on the UT Dallas campus, 800 West Campbell Road, Richardson, Texas 75080, in Arts and Humanities 1(AH1).   Parking passes will be provided for community members.

The UT Dallas Community Chorale (MUSI 2327) performs in the Fall and Spring Arts Festivals. The 40 - 80 voice chorale offers students, alums and faculty as well as members of the community, an opportunity to enjoy performing interesting choral music. Students will benefit by improving their singing and achieving musical goals through the preparation and performance of excellent and fun music. The Chorale is open to all who are interested in singing music in a wide variety of styles from Medieval to modern pop.

The mission of this Chorale is to give student singers and community singers the opportunity to enjoy performing choral music. Emphasis is placed on producing free and beautiful sounds while achieving the highest level of performance. Singers will benefit in the improvement of their singing technique and from meeting mutual musical goals with others.

The Chorale has performed the of music of Purcell, Handel, Haydn, Mozart, Beethoven, Rachmaninov, Debussy, Faure, Elgar, Howells, Britten, Bruckner, Dello Joio, Tavener, Lauridsen, Stroope, and Whitacre.
